How Does It Work!?

We use machines much like the resin printer pictured below.

The vat located on the floor of the printer has a clear bottom known as an "FEP screen/film".

UV resin, a toxic chemical, is poured into the vat.

The machine then takes a file, called an STL, and iterates through thousands of layers that make
up the desired object. Much like slices from a loaf of bread.

These layers are displayed on a screen in the floor of the printer. The light from the display causes the
resin in the vat above (remember the clear FEP screen) to harden into a mostly solid form.
The machine places an "arm" or plate in the vat for the resin to solidify onto.

As the "arm" or plate lowers and rises with each layer it creates the desired object. The object can then be
removed from the plate, washed in 97% isopropyl alcohol (or higher) and then placed under
a UV light to finish curing into a hard plastic. This process can take
hours or even days to complete.
